# Visual Audit — Consultates v2.0 (Honest Assessment)

Audited: 2026-03-07

## Overall Verdict: Serviceable first draft. Soulless.

The site is structurally correct — no broken layouts, no slop patterns. But it has no visual identity beyond the homepage hero. Most pages are white text on white backgrounds with purple headings. There's no story, no narrative arc, no visual rhythm. The homepage hero is alive; everything else is a skeleton waiting for skin.

---

## What's Actually Wrong

### 1. Almost entirely white
Outside the homepage hero and the dark CTA sections, every page is white or cream with black/gray text. No photography (except Gary's headshot on About). No illustrations. No color blocks. No visual texture. The brand has Royal Purple, Deep Royal Blue, Lavender Mist, Warm Cream — but the pages barely use them. The result is pages that feel empty and institutional.

### 2. Inconsistent application across service pages
The three service pages don't share a coherent design system:

| Element | Advisory | Training | Fractional |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hero bg | Cream, centered | Cream, left-aligned | Dark, centered |
| Hero stat | None | "30-50" badge | None |
| "What you get" | Numbered steps + connecting line | 2x2 card grid (broken icons) | 3 plain cards in a row |
| "Who it's for" | 3 cards | Bullet list | Checkmark list |
| Testimonial | Anonymous (initials) | Anonymous (initials) | Named (Frank Wiener) |
| Icons | None | Broken gray dots | None |

Three pages, three different visual treatments for the same sections. An agent built each to spec but nobody enforced a shared pattern language.

### 3. No narrative or visual storytelling
The StoryBrand framework (empathy → problem → solution → proof → action) drives the homepage but doesn't carry through to service pages. Each service page is a flat list of sections: hero, what you get, who it's for, testimonial, how it works, CTA. There's no emotional arc. No "before/after." No visual escalation. You scroll down and get the same visual weight from top to bottom.

### 4. The homepage hero set a bar nothing else meets
The hero has: dark cinematic background, letter-stagger animation, typewriter sub-copy, btn-alive CTA with glow, stats bar with stroke-draw icons. It feels crafted.

Then you scroll to the next section and it's... white background, serif heading, three cards. The energy drops off a cliff and never comes back. The dark CTA sections at the bottom of each page echo the hero, but everything between the hero and the CTA is flat.

### 5. Icons are broken or absent
- Training page: icon circles render as tiny gray dots (broken)
- Advisory page: no icons at all in "What you get"
- Fractional page: no icons at all in "What you get"
- Homepage offering cards: icons work (cream bg + purple border — fixed last session)

The icon treatment that works on the homepage offering cards hasn't been applied to service pages.

### 6. About page middle sections are visually dead
"A family business" and "Lead with AI PRO" are important content but have zero visual distinction. They're paragraphs on white. No card treatment, no accent color, no imagery. They disappear between the strong timeline above and the dark CTA below.

---

## What's Working

1. **Homepage hero** — distinctive, animated, sets the brand tone
2. **Dark CTA sections** — StaggerHeading + btn-alive--on-dark mirrors the hero. Consistent across all pages.
3. **"How it works" connecting-line sections** — clean numbered steps with purple circles and connecting line. Consistent pattern.
4. **Footer** — clean, informative, not over-designed
5. **Navigation** — simple, functional, btn-alive CTA in header
6. **No slop** — gradient buttons, pill shapes, eyebrow labels, glassmorphism all absent

---

## What Needs to Happen

### Visual identity needs to flow from the hero downward
The hero establishes: dark backgrounds, animation, purple accents, cinematic feel. The rest of the site needs to carry that energy — not match it 1:1, but exist in the same visual universe.

### Service pages need a shared pattern language
One template, three content fills. Same section types, same visual treatments, differentiated by content and accent details — not by layout structure.

### Color and texture need to show up between hero and CTA
Right now: dark hero → white/cream sections → dark CTA. The middle is a visual desert. Needs: accent color blocks, imagery, illustrations, testimonial cards with visual weight, stat callouts, anything that breaks the monotony.

### Icons need to work everywhere or nowhere
Either commit to the cream-bg + purple-border icon containers on all service page cards, or remove them. Half-working is worse than absent.

### The content slots (whitespace) need a plan
Gary confirmed these are intentional — reserved for rotating quotes, stats, and contextual content. But right now they're empty, which amplifies the "soulless" feeling. Even placeholder content or a design pattern (subtle divider, accent line, section marker) would help.

---

## Summary
The site has good bones: clean structure, no technical debt, no slop. The homepage hero and dark CTAs prove the brand can express well. The problem is that 80% of the page area (the middle sections) has no visual life. It reads as a wireframe with real copy pasted in.
